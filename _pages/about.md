Let me quickly introduce myself: My name is **Aashish Yadavally**, first name pronounced  /	ɑ:ʃi:ʃ /.

I am a recent Master's graduate in Artificial Intelligence at the University of Georgia. My Master's thesis focused on employing numerical weather prediction (NWP) models for the purpose of day-ahead solar irradiance forecasting. I received my bachelor's degree in Computer Science from Indian Institute of Information Technology (IIIT) Vadodara, India.

Prior to joining the *Institute for Artificial Intelligence* at the University of Georgia as a Graduate Research Assistant under [Dr. Frederick Maier](https://csci.franklin.uga.edu/directory/people/fred-maier) and [Dr. Khaled Rasheed](http://cobweb.cs.uga.edu/~khaled/), I interned as an Undergraduate Research Assistant under [Dr. Anil Vuppula](https://www.iiit.ac.in/people/faculty/anilvuppala/) at the *Speech Research Lab* at IIIT, Hyderabad, India. My work there included exploring various deep learning based acoustic modeling techniques for the purpose of Automatic Speech Recognition.

My current research interests include the fields of natural language processing and speech processing, and I am particularly intrigued by the prospects of incorporating linguistic elements in the construction of language representations (or better, language-independent representations) for the purpose of efficient language understanding.

I encourage everyone to reach out to me with any questions, remarks, suggestions, or even if to just share something you find interesting!
